More Information About the Socioeconomic Peace Index Tool

The Socio-Economic Peacebuilding Index (SEPI) is a composite indicator developed by the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) Istanbul International Centre for Private Sector in Development (ICPSD). It measures structural socio-economic conditions associated with conflict vulnerability in the Horn of Africa, with a focus on Somalia, South Sudan, and Kenya.

SEPI is grounded in relative deprivation theory, which states that the gap between perceived entitlements and actual conditions can create grievances that escalate into collective violence under certain socioeconomic contexts.

SEPI is calculated at the Admin-1 level. A high SEPI score reflects better structural conditions relative to other regions in the same country. Scores are relative within country and are not a universal standard.

What Does the Map Show?

Each Admin-1 region is shaded from lower capacity to higher capacity relative to peer regions in the same country. Five pillars drive the composite score:

Pillar What it captures
Education Index School attendance, literacy, school access, teacher presence
Food Security Index Share of population in IPC Crisis or worse (Phase 3+)
Poverty Reduction Index Poverty headcount, income/expenditure per capita
Health Access Index Healthcare access, health facility density, hospital density
Climate Resilience Index Vegetation health, soil moisture, drought severity

Hovering over or clicking a region reveals its SEPI score, within-country rank, and pillar scores.

Methodology

SEPI integrates multiple indicators of structural socio-economic capacity to create a comparative assessment tool for peacebuilding programming.

  1. Data Collection: Household surveys, remote sensing, and international databases.
  2. Indicator Screening: Candidate indicators are reviewed for domain coverage, quality, and independence.
  3. Normalization and Polarity Alignment: Indicators are rescaled to a common [0,1] range.
  4. Pillar Score Calculation: Indicators are grouped into five pillars with equal within-pillar weights.
  5. SEPI Composite Score: Pillars are combined with a geometric mean and equal pillar weights.
  6. Validation: Tested against ACLED conflict intensity and IOM displacement tracking data.
  7. Visualization: Results are published via interactive Admin-1 maps and dashboards.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why use a geometric mean across pillars instead of a simple average?

The geometric mean ensures all five dimensions matter and prevents strong performance in one pillar from fully masking very low performance in another.

How are the sub-indicators weighted?

Each pillar contributes equally to the composite score. Within each pillar, indicators are averaged with equal weights for transparency and reproducibility.

Are scores comparable across Kenya, Somalia, and South Sudan?

No. Scores are min-max normalized within each country, so a value from one country is not directly comparable to the same value in another country.

What years does the data cover?

Data sources span approximately 2019-2026, depending on indicator type and country source systems.

Is conflict data included in the SEPI score?

No. Conflict data is used for validation after index construction, not as an input to the composite SEPI score.
